## The promise: every inquiry answered in under 60 seconds, around the clock

Right now, when a customer fills out a form on your website or texts your number, the average dealership takes **more than five hours** to respond. By that time, 78% of them have already bought from whoever answered first. **Our system changes that.** Every lead gets a thoughtful, personalized response in under 60 seconds — at lunch, in the evening, on a Sunday, on Christmas.

That's the one thing to remember about the whole product. Everything else on this page is what makes it work without breaking.

## 1. The customer reaches you, however they want

### Website contact form
The moment a customer hits "Submit" on your website, they receive a text on their phone. The text is signed by your dealership, addresses them by name, and references the specific vehicle they asked about. They're not left wondering "did they get my message?"

### Texting your main number
When a customer texts your dealership's phone number, they get the same kind of instant, personal response — even if your team is at lunch, in a meeting, or asleep. The number stays the same. The experience stays the same.

### Missed call text-back
If a customer calls and no one picks up — because you're on another line, after hours, or it's a busy Saturday — they automatically receive a text saying "Hi! We missed your call to [your dealership]. Text us here or call back. We'll get back to you right away." Most dealers lose these leads to silence. You don't.

### Email inquiries (if you use AutoTrader, CarGurus, Kijiji, etc.)
When a customer sends a question through any of your listing sites, it gets routed into the same system. The customer gets the same instant text reply with the same personalized touch. No lead falls through the cracks because they used a different channel.

## 2. A real conversation, not a robot

When the customer replies — and they will, because you just texted them first — your sales assistant takes over the conversation. It is:

- **Trained on your actual inventory** — it can only mention cars you really have, with the right price, mileage, and features. It cannot make up a vehicle. If you don't have a green Tucson, it will not invent one.
- **Aware of your business hours** — during the day, the assistant will suggest a sales rep will follow up. After hours, it can keep the conversation going so the customer doesn't drift to a competitor while they wait for Tuesday morning.
- **Tone-matched to your dealership** — friendly and low-pressure, never pushy, never negotiating on price. The price conversation happens face-to-face when the customer comes in.

Your customer feels like they're talking to a helpful person at your dealership, not a chatbot. They get answers to "What SUVs do you have under $30,000?" or "Does the Tucson come in blue?" without waiting for someone to call them back tomorrow.

And — importantly — **it remembers the conversation.** If the customer texted you about a RAV4 yesterday and asks "what about the CR-V?" today, the assistant knows who they are, what they've already seen, and what they're shopping for. They don't have to start over.

---

## 3. A test drive booked over text

The assistant can offer specific time slots — "How about Tuesday at 2pm, or Wednesday at 10am?" — and book the test drive right there in the conversation. No back-and-forth. No "let me check with Mike and get back to you."

The customer walks away from the text exchange with a **confirmed appointment on the calendar.** Your sales rep sees it immediately in the dashboard, with the customer's full conversation history attached. Your rep walks into the appointment already knowing what the customer wants — no awkward "so, what were you looking at?" re-introduction.

If the customer wants to reschedule, the same conversation handles it.

---

## 4. The customer is always in control

### Opt-out works instantly
If the customer texts **STOP**, they receive an immediate confirmation ("You have been unsubscribed. Reply START to come back.") and never receive another message from your dealership unless they choose to come back.

This isn't a checkbox in a policy document that someone might forget about. It is enforced in code, on every message, every time. Required by Canadian law (CASL). Done correctly so you don't get fined.

### Quiet hours respected
We will not text customers between 9 PM and 8 AM in their time zone. The only exceptions are: a reply to a message the customer just sent us (because they texted us first), and a STOP confirmation (which legally must happen immediately, no matter the hour).

### Resubscribe is one word
If a customer comes back later and texts **START**, they're resubscribed and the conversation picks up where it left off. No forms to fill, no friction.

---

## 5. Their data is private, by default

We collect only what we need to follow up: name, phone number, the vehicle they're interested in. Every message, every consent decision, every opt-out is logged and timestamped. The full record is kept for seven years, as required by Canadian anti-spam law for audit purposes.

We do not sell, share, or leak customer data. The customer's information is visible only to your dealership. If they ever ask "what do you have on me?", you can show them — and you can delete it on request, as required by BC privacy law (PIPA).

## 6. One customer, one conversation — across every channel

If a customer first texts you about a RAV4 on Monday, then submits a web form about a Civic on Wednesday, then calls you on Friday and no one answers — the system treats all three as the same person. They don't have to re-explain what they're looking for. Your sales rep sees the full history, in chronological order, the moment they open the lead.

This is the difference between talking to a small dealership and talking to a big box. Your customers feel **known** — even if your team only sees them once.
