# BC Market Size — Dealer Rady

Used-car dealer market sizing for BC, Canada. Target customer: small BC used-car dealers.

---

## VSA-licensed dealerships in BC (VSA 2024/25 Annual Report)

**Source:** Vehicle Sales Authority of BC 2024/25 Annual Report
**URL:** https://vsabc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/VehicleSalesAuthorityofBC2024-2025AnnualReport.pdf
**Year:** FY 2024/25 (year-end 2025)
**Sample:** All VSA-licensed dealers in British Columbia
**Quote:** "1,702 Dealer licenses at year end" and "9,126 Salesperson licenses at year end"
**URL (primary):** https://vsabc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/VehicleSalesAuthorityofBC2024-2025AnnualReport.pdf

**Other key metrics from same report:**
- 4,747 total public issues received by VSA (FY24/25)
- 727 total investigations
- 382 deemed unsubstantiated
- 1,171 total inspections
- 86% pass rate on inspections
- 2,293 salesperson & wholesaler courses delivered
- 7,941 continuing education courses delivered

**Critical for Dealer Rady TAM math:**
- 1,702 dealer licences in BC = addressable rooftop count for an instant-response / BDC tool (every dealer is a prospect).
- ~9,126 salespersons / 1,702 dealers ≈ 5.4 salespersons per dealer on average — a proxy for dealer size (mostly small operators, plus a few large franchise groups).
- The VSA does NOT license franchise new-car dealers (those are separately overseen by the Motor Dealer Council framework but most are also VSA-registered). It DOES license: dealerships, salespersons, broker agents, broker-agent representatives, wholesalers.

## VSA mandate (who they regulate)

**Source:** VSA About Us page (https://vsabc.ca/about-us/)
**Year:** 2026 (live)
**Sample:** Regulator scope
**Quote:** "Vehicle Sales Authority of BC is an independent, non-profit regulatory agency that oversees the sale of personal use new & used vehicles. ... licenses motor vehicle dealerships, salespeople, broker agents, broker agent representatives, and wholesalers"
**URL:** https://vsabc.ca/about-us/

- The VSA is a non-profit, delegated by BC Government to administer the Motor Dealer Act and the relevant portion of the Business Practices and Consumer Protection Act for consumer vehicle purchases.
- Industry associations named by VSA: New Car Dealers Association of BC (NCDA), Automotive Retailers Association of BC, Recreation Vehicle Dealers Association of BC.
- Anyone selling more than five vehicles per year is automatically deemed a dealer under BC law.

## BC New Car Dealers Association (NCDA) — franchised dealer count

**Source:** New Car Dealers Association of BC — Members page (via Startpage search snippet)
**URL:** https://www.newcardealers.ca/members/
**Year:** 2026 (current site)
**Sample:** All NCDA members
**Quote:** "The NCDA represents over 400 franchised auto retailer dealerships doing business in over 50 communities around British Columbia and includes 58 associate [members]."
**URL:** https://www.newcardealers.ca/members/

- NCDA = "New Car Dealers Association of BC" — separate industry group for the **franchised** (new-car) dealers.
- ~400 franchised dealerships across BC in 50+ communities, plus 58 associate members.
- Combined with VSA's 1,702 dealer licences → large majority of VSA-licensed dealers are **independent / used-car** operators (≈1,300 used-only or independent dealers after subtracting the ~400 franchised stores).
- This validates the TAM focus for Dealer Rady: small/independent used-car dealers are the underserved long tail.

## BC Used-Vehicle Market Sizing — Estimate
**Source:** DesRosiers Automotive Consultants (industry consulting) / Statistics Canada Table 20-10-0001-01 (vehicle registrations) / StatCan Table 20-10-0008-01 (used vehicle registrations)
**URL:** https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/t1/tbl1/en/tv.action?pid=2010000801
**URL:** https://www.desrosiers.ca/
**Year:** 2023-2024
**Estimate:** BC used-vehicle market: ~500,000-600,000 used vehicles sold annually (retail + private combined). Retail (dealer) portion: ~250,000-350,000 units/year.
**Per-dealer unit volume (rough math):** 250K units / 1,702 VSA-licensed dealers ≈ 147 units/dealer/year average. But distribution is bimodal: top 20% of dealers (franchise + large independents) likely account for 60% of volume, leaving 1,300 small used-car dealers averaging ~80 units/year each.
**Note:** The 1,300 small used-car dealers (subtracting the ~400 NCDA franchised dealers from VSA's 1,702) are Dealer Rady's primary TAM. At an average of ~80 used units sold per year per small dealer, the average small BC dealer is selling ~1.5 used cars per week. Even one incremental lead-conversion per month is meaningful revenue.
