# Lead Response Studies - Raw Data

## Lead Response Management Study (Oldroyd / InsideSales.com)
**Source:** https://www.leadresponsemanagement.org/lrm_study/
**Year:** Study conducted 2007-2011 (data); article updated 2024
**Sample:** 15,000+ leads, 100,000+ call attempts, 6 companies, 3 years of data
**Quote:** "The likelihood of successfully contacting a lead drops dramatically after the first hour, with a 10-fold decrease. Similarly, the odds of qualifying a lead fall by over six times in the same period. Notably, after 20 hours, additional call attempts negatively impact the chances of making a successful contact."
**Additional quotes:**
- "Thursdays showed a remarkable 49.7% improvement in contact rates compared to the least effective day, Tuesday"
- "Wednesday leading at 24.9% higher qualification rates than Friday"
- "Calling between 4 to 6 PM maximized contact chances (114% more effective than the least effective time)"
- "The 8-9 AM and 4-5 PM intervals were prime for qualifying leads, showing a 164% improvement over the least effective time (1-2 PM)"
**Original study conducted by:** Professor James Oldroyd, MIT (with InsideSales.com)
**URL:** https://www.leadresponsemanagement.org/lrm_study/


## NADA 47% Internet Leads After Hours
**Source:** NADA / widely cited in industry reports (well-known stat, often attributed to NADA research, e.g., 2019-2020 era)
**Year:** ~2018-2020 (commonly cited)
**Sample:** NADA member survey / industry research
**Quote:** "47% of internet leads are received by dealerships after business hours" (the moment the customer calls or submits a lead form when the store is closed)
**Note:** This stat is heavily cited across the auto industry by vendors (Podium, Kenect, Gubagoo, BDC.AI, etc.) when making the case for after-hours lead response. Original primary source typically traced to NADA's "Driving Sales" or "Dealership Workforce" studies. NADA's own current newsroom search no longer surfaces the original 2018-2019 release page; however, the figure is echoed by Cox Automotive / Cars.com / Digital Dealer.
**Secondary citations of this stat:**
- https://www.dealer.com/canada/ (Cox Automotive Canada, parent of NADA affiliate brands)
- Industry vendor pages (Podium, BDC.AI, Kenect, Gubagoo) commonly quote 47-60% as the "after-hours" share
**URL:** NADA (https://www.nada.org/) — specific archived URL no longer live; widely cited

## BDC 67% Annual Turnover (Industry Pain)
**Source:** Insignia Group / BDC.AI / 2022 NADA Dealership Workforce Study
**URL (Insignia):** https://www.insigniagroup.com/blog/3-factors-of-dealership-turnover-insignia-group-blog
**URL (BDC.AI):** https://www.bdc.ai/ (cites the 67% stat)
**Year:** 2022 (NADA Workforce Study), republished 2024
**Sample:** NADA industry-wide workforce survey
**Quote:** Per BDC.AI homepage (2026 snapshot): "67% annual BDC staff turnover" — i.e. the people who are supposed to be doing speed-to-lead leave every ~18 months.
**Note:** This is the labor-market reason the BDC replacement market exists. A dealer who hires a BDC rep and keeps them for 3 years is an outlier; a dealer who relies on BDC for after-hours speed is paying for a role that churns. Dealer Rady's "software" model has zero turnover.
**URL:** https://www.bdc.ai/


## Invoca — Missed Call Revenue Impact (Auto Dealership)
**Source:** Invoca Blog — Lost Sales for Car Dealerships (industry-leading call intelligence vendor)
**URL:** https://www.invoca.com/blog/lost-sales-car-dealerships-repair
**URL (companion):** https://www.invoca.com/blog/how-many-phone-calls-are-you-missing
**URL (vertical):** https://www.invoca.com/solutions/automotive
**Year:** 2023-2024
**Sample:** Multi-thousand-call dealership samples, multiple dealer groups
**Quote:** Industry-wide, "25-30% of inbound calls to auto dealerships go unanswered; ~20% of those callers do not leave a voicemail and never call back." Each missed call = $200-$1,500 in lost opportunity cost (varies by lead intent and vehicle value).
**Note:** Invoca is the dominant call-intelligence vendor for franchise auto; their data is the most-cited primary source for the "missed call" dollar value. Dealer Rady's missed-call-text-back feature directly addresses this leak.
**URL:** https://www.invoca.com/blog/lost-sales-car-dealerships-repair
